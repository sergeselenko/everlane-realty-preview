## Planned structure

1. **Why is this so confusing?** The one-paragraph legal frame: FL §509.032(7)(b) preempts
   duration/frequency regulation — EXCEPT ordinances adopted on or before June 1, 2011,
   which are grandfathered (verified July 2026, kb/facts/str-rules.yaml). That grandfather
   clause is the whole map: every city's answer depends on what it had on the books in 2011.
2. **The matrix (the guide's core asset).** One row per municipality: St. Petersburg ·
   Gulfport · St. Pete Beach · Treasure Island · Madeira Beach · Clearwater · unincorporated
   Pinellas. Columns: minimum-stay/frequency rule · zoning where allowed · registration/
   inspection requirements · fines · ordinance number + adoption date + last-checked date.
   EVERY CELL cites the municipality's current ordinance with its date; empty cell says
   "verify with the city" — never a guess.
3. **St. Petersburg in detail.** The pre-2011 (2001) rule: sub-30-day rentals max 3×/12mo in
   residential zones; downtown/mixed-use carve-outs; the super-fine alignment (str-rules.yaml,
   MEDIUM — exact LDR section numbers pulled from Municode at drafting).
4. **What every operator needs regardless of city.** DBPR vacation-rental license, state
   sales tax + Pinellas tourist development tax, BTR. (str-rules.yaml, verify at drafting.)
5. **What this means for a purchase.** Rule-risk as a price factor; condo/HOA private
   restrictions trump everything (they're not preempted); the honest "rules churn" caveat.
