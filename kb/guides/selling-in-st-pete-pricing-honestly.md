## Planned structure

1. **Why is the online estimate wrong about my house?** AVMs in one honest paragraph: what
   they're good at (homogeneous stock) and why St. Pete breaks them — flood-zone variance
   lot-to-lot, pre-war renovation spread, elevation as invisible value. Cite the AVM
   vendors' OWN published accuracy/error bands (Zillow publishes Zestimate error rates —
   cite the current page with date, use their numbers, not ours).
2. **CMA vs. appraisal vs. AVM — who's measuring what?** Three instruments, three purposes,
   one table.
3. **What actually moves the number here?** Elevation certificate / flood zone, the 49%-rule
   renovation ledger (kb/facts/flood.yaml), insurance assumability of the buyer's quote,
   post-2024 elevated-vs-not spread. Every claim either sourced or labeled broker judgment.
4. **What does an honest range look like?** Why we deliver a range + confidence, never a
   point estimate — and why the broker signs it (the valuation surface's promise: a human
   gate, ≤24h).
5. **When is the answer "don't sell yet"?** The honest cases — labeled first-hand judgment.
