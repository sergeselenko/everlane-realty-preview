## Planned structure

1. **What's the actual sequence?** Offer anatomy in this market (AS-IS contract norm,
   escrow, effective date), inspection period as the real decision window, appraisal,
   clear-to-close. Told as the timeline we actually run, with the local failure points.
2. **What does it cost beyond the price?** The full closing-cost ledger for Pinellas:
   doc stamps on the deed (who customarily pays what locally), title insurance & who picks
   the agent here, lender costs, prepaid insurance/taxes escrow. Honest ranges cited to
   current rate cards — never invented averages.
3. **What's different about St. Pete specifically?** The flood layer on every offer (link
   flagship guide), the 49% rule as a renovation ceiling (kb/facts/flood.yaml), insurance
   quotes BEFORE waiving contingencies, milestone-inspection context for condos (tranche-2
   guide candidate), the 2024-storm disclosure reality.
4. **What does the inspection period really decide?** 4-point + wind mitigation as insurance
   documents, not formalities; sewer laterals in the pre-war districts; what "AS-IS" does and
   does not mean in Florida.
5. **How do offers actually win here?** The broker's read — labeled as professional judgment,
   not statistics (any market number renders from the live pipeline or not at all).
